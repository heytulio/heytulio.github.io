The Proposal of Law to Regulate AI

Regulations of AI on Brazil

The proposal, initially made by the senator Rodrigo Pacheco, to regulate the use of artificial intelligences has been under discussion on Brazil since 2022, given that there are already other existing proposals in other countries. The aim is to establish criteria for use and also appropriate sanctions for those who violate the laws attributed to the bill.

Since July 2023, the texts that would regulate this matter have been discussed in the Chamber of Deputies, as it is a facilitating tool but currently has uncontrolled use. Thus, the parameters used for regulation include transparency, data security, and protection of copyright, a topic widely discussed by online communities

"The European experience in regulating artificial intelligence can serve as a global reference and was discussed this Thursday (23) in the Communication and Digital Law Commission (CCDD). Precision and the reduction of productivity costs are among the main benefits of artificial intelligence acknowledged in the regulatory model, which also anticipates that the violation of rights should be safeguarded with specific mechanisms such as external audits. The European model is expected to contribute to the development of legislation on artificial intelligence in Brazil."
via Agência Senado

On November 1st of this year, a public hearing for the consultation of the bill (PL) was initiated, and it remains open until November 25, 2023. This hearing aims to propose the entire regulation of the use of these artificial intelligences (IAs). It is important that everyone participates in this public consultation, which seeks to defend all our rights.
